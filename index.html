Many drummers play shows, but no one does it as much as Rod Breland. With inspirations such as Kevin Brunson and Marvin Mcquitty -RIP, Rod has led a life full of inspiration and knowledge to the drum community. Rod credit’s his father Mickey Breland for getting his interest in becoming a drummer. Rod’s incredible ability to reach people and communicate, mixed with his sense of humor, provides him with an ability to reach every person from any style. His achievements as a drummer go beyond that of teachings, being a professional drummer since the age of 12.

Rod started drumming early in his life with his father. He used to have jam sessions with his father on Alto Saxophone in the living room. Rod always had an outgoing personality, which made him an easy musician to work with. He knew right away that he would be a drummer.

Rod has quite the education to back him up. Starting as early as he could, he has taken drum lessons from many professionals. These include lessons from drum masters like Fred Houge, Jody Dunwoody, and Ed Fierris. He spent many years studying drum rudiments, theory, gospel and other techniques from these drumming greats. In the year of 1996, Rod attended Mississippi Valley State University where he furthered his drumming knowledge under the direction of many drumming instructors.

Rod is best known for his work as a performer. When he was starting out, he performed with the Martin Luther King Jr. Celebration Choir in Gulfport, MS.. His popularity grew as a drummer r as he played at his church, First Missionary Baptist Church in Hansboro (Gulfport, MS), Harrison Central High School (Lyman, MS), Marching Band, Drumline, and Concert Band Mississippi Valley State University (Itta Bena, MS) Mean Green Machine Marching Band, Jazz Band, Choir, Steel Drum Band, and Percussion Ensemble. This is just a small sample of the many shows he has played. Rod now performs in Atlanta, GA on several stages. He is the new drummer of Frankie’s Blues Mission (Atlanta, GA). He is also the drummer for Greater Fair Hill Baptist Church (Atlanta, GA). He has performed behind Fantasia, Keith Sweat,Lil G, Terri Carroll, LaMorris Sellers, and Miss Miller. He also played with 12:01 Entertainment, Willie Hill, Eric Thomas, and Reec.

If you haven’t heard of Rod Breland yet, you may want to take the time to check him out. His incredible ability to perform will inspire anyone. Being an easy going and reliable beast, Rod has a special place in the new music scene today. Plus, with teachings from Fred Houge, Jody Dunwoody, and Ed Fierris, Rod sure has a lot of experience behind him. Rod plans on playing the drums until the end of his life, following in the footsteps of Max Roach.
